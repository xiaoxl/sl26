Problem 1: Bias-Variance Decomposition

Suppose the data are generated from the model

\[ Y = f(X) + \varepsilon, \]

where

\[ E(\varepsilon \mid X)=0, \qquad \mathrm{Var}(\varepsilon \mid X)=\sigma^2. \]

Let \(\hat{f}(x_0)\) be an estimator of \(f(x_0)\) based on a training sample, and let

\[ Y_0 = f(x_0) + \varepsilon_0 \]

be a future response at the same predictor value \(x_0\).

  1. Show that the expected prediction error at \(x_0\) is

    \[ E\big[(Y_0-\hat f(x_0))^2\big]. \]

  2. Derive the decomposition

    \[ E\big[(Y_0-\hat f(x_0))^2\big] = \mathrm{Var}(\hat f(x_0)) + \big(E[\hat f(x_0)]-f(x_0)\big)^2 + \sigma^2. \]

  3. Clearly explain the meaning of the three terms in this decomposition.

Problem 2: OLS Unbiasedness and Variance

Consider the linear model

\[ \mathbf{y} = \mathbf{X}\boldsymbol{\beta} + \boldsymbol{\varepsilon}, \]

where

\[ E(\boldsymbol{\varepsilon})=\mathbf{0}, \qquad \mathrm{Var}(\boldsymbol{\varepsilon})=\sigma^2 \mathbf{I}. \]

Assume \(\mathbf{X}\) has full column rank.

  1. Show that the ordinary least squares estimator is

    \[ \hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}} = (\mathbf{X}^\top \mathbf{X})^{-1}\mathbf{X}^\top \mathbf{y}. \]

  2. Prove that \(\hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}}\) is unbiased.

  3. Show that

    \[ \mathrm{Var}(\hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}}) = \sigma^2(\mathbf{X}^\top \mathbf{X})^{-1}. \]

  4. Briefly explain why highly correlated predictors can inflate the variance of \(\hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}}\).

Problem 6: Simulation of OLS under a Correctly Specified Model

In this problem, you will examine the sampling variability of OLS.

Generate data from

\[ y_i = 3 + 2x_i + \varepsilon_i, \qquad \varepsilon_i \sim N(0,1), \]

with \(n=100\) and \(x_i \sim \mathrm{Unif}(0,1)\).

  1. Write R code to generate one dataset and fit the model \(y \sim x\).
  2. Repeat the simulation 500 times.
  3. Store the estimated slope and intercept from each repetition.
  4. Plot histograms of the 500 estimated slopes and intercepts.
  5. Compute the empirical mean and empirical standard deviation of the estimated slope.
  6. Compare the empirical mean of the slope estimator with the true value \(2\).
  7. Briefly comment on whether the simulation agrees with the theoretical unbiasedness of OLS.
